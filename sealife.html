Green Sea Turtle

The Green Sea Turtle (Chelonia Mydas), have been endangered officially since 1978, although their population has been on the decline over the last century. The loss of their habitat remains as one of the biggest threats to their existence, along with the illegal trading market.

Hammerhead Shark

The Hammerhead Shark (Sphyrnidae), known for their unique and humorous shape, have resided primarily in the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Ocenas, as well as the Mediterranean sea. Officially endangered since 2006, their population is below 200. They are threatened by "commercial fishing" for the sale on the shark fin trade market.

Monk Seals

The Monk Seal (Monachini), are mostly threatened by toxoplasmosis; an infection of a parasite. With their population at a staggering 1,500, he result of their potential extinction would cost the Hawaiian ecosystem greatly as they play a powerful role in the food chain. As an apex predator, they are heavily relied on to keep crustacean, fish, and squid populations from becoming out of control.

Chilean Dolphin

The Chilean Dolphin (Cephalorhynchus eutropia), residing in Chile, has not been seen in over 20 years, however, there are approximately 5,000 still living. Approximately 1,500 are killed a year for the use of bait for swordfishing and crab fisheries.
